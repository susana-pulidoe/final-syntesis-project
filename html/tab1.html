Solar System

- What is it? -

The solar system is a planetary system.
A planetary system is made up of a star and the celestial bodies that revolve around it
In the case of our solar system, the star that shapes the entire planetary system is the Sun, which occupies more than 30 billion kilometers, in which, as we said, its eight planets and other celestial objects are located.

The next most important objects of the solar system are the planets, which we can divide into two different classes. Thus, Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars are smaller planets, due to their position in the solar system known as interior and rocky planets. There are also Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune which are outer planets, much larger and composed of gas.


Apart from the planets, 5 of the so-called dwarf planets are also known in the solar system. Ceres, located in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, and Pluto, Haumea, Makemake and Eris, located in the Kuiper belt.

The solar system is part of our galaxy, the Milky Way, a barred spiral-type galaxy that has a diameter of approximately 105,000 light-years between its most distant ends. It is located in the Orion or Local arm, which is part of the Sagittarius spiral arm.


Jupiter's Moons

Jupiter's moons are the set of natural satellites that orbit this giant planet of the outer solar system.
So far, the existence of about 79 moons of Jupiter is known, which makes it the planet with the largest entourage in the solar system.

The four largest moons are called the "Galilean satellites", since they were discovered in 1610 by the famous Galileo Galilei.

The best known are Io, Europa and Calisto.
But the biggest one is Ganymede.
