Oort Cloud

In the most remote place of our Solar System, there is the Oort Cloud.

Unlike many planets and stars, this cloud is a spherical shell that surrounds it completely.
Made of thousands or maybe millions of pieces of icy space bodies, it has a much larger extension than the planetary region of the solar system and is almost a year's light from the sun.

It was first studied in 1950, the name of the cloud is due to the astronomer Jan Hendrik Oort, who began the investigation of this region of the Solar System that would later be known by its name.

Initially, the elements that make up the cloud were part of the stars that surrounded the Sun.
When the planets were formed 4.6 billion years ago, they gave off pieces of space matter called planetesimal throughout the universe. These objects had very diverse destinies. While some were expelled from the Solar System, others remained inside, it can be said that the cloud is made up of the space matter resulting from this process.
