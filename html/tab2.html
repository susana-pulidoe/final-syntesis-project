Gravity In The Planets

- Gravity -

Gravity is what makes pieces of matter unite with each other, to form planets, moons and stars.


Gravity in the planets:

- Mercury: 3.7  m/s²
- Venus: 8.87  m/s²
- Earth: 9,807  m/s²
- Mars: 3,721  m/s²
- Jupiter: 24.79  m/s²
- Saturn: 10.44  m/s²
- Uranus: 8.87  m/s²
- Neptune: 11.15  m/s²
- Pluto, which is a dwarf planet, is 0.62  m/s²


Gravity on the moon:

The gravity of this is 1.62 m/s2, which is why an object or person on the moon would weigh 16.5% of its weight on earth.
Therefore, a person would be much lighter on the moon.




Gravity of the sun:

The gravity is 274 m/s2, it is more intense than the earth.
That means that on the surface of the Sun you would weigh 27.9 times what you weigh on Earth.



But the force of gravity is not only responsible for the fall of the apple on a planet, it is also responsible for the planets rotating around the Sun attracting each other with a gravitational interaction.
